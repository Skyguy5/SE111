Shaman

Similar to Druids, Shamans embrace the beauty of nature and also practice the more chaotic aspects of it. Flinging their fury through spells or their weapons, Shamans are unbound in nature and embody all of nature's wrath, as well as it's serenity.

You must have a Wisdom score and Charisma score of 13 or higher in order to multiclass in or out of this class.

Class Features

As a shaman, you gain the following class features.

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d8 per shaman level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per shaman level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: Light armor, medium armor, shields
Weapons: Clubs, daggers, maces, quarterstaffs, sickles
Tools: Herbalism kit
Saving Throws: Charisma, Wisdom
Skills: Choose two from Arcana, Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Religion, and Survival

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
• (a) a wooden shield or (b) any simple weapon
• (a) a mace or (b) any simple melee weapon
• Leather armor, an explorer's pack, and a shamanistic focus

Shamanistic

Your connection with the elements has given you the ability to read, write, and speak Primordial.

Spellcasting

Drawing on the chaotic essence of nature itself, you can cast spells to shape that essence to your will.

Cantrips

At 1st level, you know two cantrips of your choice from the shaman spell list. You learn additional Shaman cantrips of your choice at higher levels, as shown in the Cantrips Known column of the Shaman table.

Preparing and Casting Spells

The Shaman table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your spells of 1st level and higher. Different subclasses have access to different spell slots (Enhancement specifically has no spell slots above sixth level). To cast one of these shaman spells, you must expend a slot of the spell's level or higher. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a long rest.

You prepare the list of shaman spells that are available for you to cast, choosing from the shaman spell list. When you do so, choose a number of shaman spells equal to your Wisdom modifier + your Shaman level (minimum of one spell). The spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

For example, if you are a 3rd-level shaman, you have four 1st-level and two 2nd-level spell slots. With a Wisdom of 16, your list of prepared spells can include six spells of 1st or 2nd level, in any combination. If you prepare the 1st-level spell Cure Wounds, you can cast it using a 1st-level or 2nd-level slot. Casting the spell doesn't remove it from your list of prepared spells.
You can also change your list of prepared spells when you finish a long rest. Preparing a new list of shaman spells requires time spent in evocation: at least 1 minute per spell level for each spell on your list.

Spellcasting Ability

Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for your shaman spells, since your magic draws upon your devotion and attunement to the elements. You use your Wisdom whenever a spell refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your Wisdom modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a shaman spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Ritual Casting

You can cast a shaman spell as a ritual if that spell has the ritual tag and you have the spell prepared.

Spellcasting Focus

You can use a shamanistic focus as a spellcasting focus for your shaman spells.

Totem Walker

Starting at 2nd level, your connection with the elements has allowed you to infuse inanimate objects with the power of nature.

In order to infuse an object with this power, you must spend one hour funneling your essence into the object. The object must be no larger than 1 foot in height, and must be made of wood or iron. To infuse an object, you must spend one hour and expend the item related to that totem as shown in the table below. Once an object is infused with an element, it can only ever be that element unless a new totem is infused (the old totem is destroyed in the process).

You may only have one totem infusion at any given time, and when you use an infused totem, you must spend an hour re-infusing it before it can be used again without material components.

At 3rd level, you choose to identify with a group of Shamans. Your choice grants you features at 2nd level and again at 6th, 10th, and 14th level.

Weapon Imbues

At 3rd level, you gain the ability to imbue your weapon with magical properties that benefit your abilities in combat.

To imbue a weapon, you must spend one hour of concentration performing a ritual (no components required) on the weapon, which must be in the center of a triangle drawn in any organic body (Dirt, Sand, Carved in stone).
You may only imbue one heavy weapon, or two light weapons at a time. When you imbue a weapon, you become attuned to it.

When you imbue a weapon, you become attuned to it. This does not count towards your total attunement count.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Cantrip Versatility (Optional)

Whenever you reach a level in this class that grants the Ability Score Improvement feature, you can replace one cantrip you learned from this class's Spellcasting feature with another cantrip from the shaman spell list.

Reincarnation

Beginning at 14th level, your connection with the elements allows you to draw in its energy even on the verge of death. When you are reduced to 0 health or below, you instead are reduced to 1 health instead. You may only use this ability once per dawn.

Totemic Revelation

Beginning at 18th level, the bond between you and the elements has empowered your totems emmensely. Whenever a totem becomes active, it summons an elemental associated with the type of totem (an earth totem summons an earth elemental, a fire totem summons a fire elemental, etc).

The elemental is friendly to you and your allies, and you control all of the actions it takes. The elemental takes its turn directly after yours. Use that elementals creature sheet for its abilities, AC, health and other effects.

Ascendance

At 20th level, you gain the ability to transform your body into an incarnation of primal elemental fury. For 1 minute, you gain the following benefits:

You become immune to fire, lightning, thunder, and bludgeoning damage. You also have immunity to attacks from non-magical weapons.

You may cast the following spells without expending a spell slot, at their base levels only: Chain lightning, Healing Rain, Earthen Shield, Earthquake, and Maelstrom Strike.

Your weapon attacks are infused with swirling elemental energy, granting you an additional attack action per turn for the duration of Ascendance.
